Former HCPSS Superintendent Michael Hickey passed away Aug. 28 at the age of 84. He was Superintendent for 16 years, from July 1984 to June 2000, after which he served 18 years as a Distinguished Professor and Director of the Towson University Center for Leadership in Education. A statement from current Superintendent Dr. Michael Martirano is shared online.

" }} {{> news-item title="Evening School Registration Open Through Sept. 23" message="

Families may request enrollment in fall semester and year-long courses through the Innovative Pathways Evening Program through 6 p.m. on Friday, Sept. 23. Enrollment requests should be submitted online through HCPSS Connect Synergy and do not guarantee placement in a course.

" }} {{> news-item title="Student Meal Prices" message="

For the 2022-2023 school year, students whose families do not qualify for free or reduced lunch will be required to pay the daily meal price. Families can apply for free and reduced meals online. A new application is required every year to be eligible for free and reduced meals.

" }} {{> news-item title="Student Device Obligations" message="

All families will be provided one-time forgiveness for damaged HCPSS devices or power adapters. Should another device or power adapter become damaged, families may be held responsible at reduced-tier rates. Lost or stolen devices and power adapters are not part of the one-time forgiveness. Students who have an outstanding device obligation will be provided a device for use at school until the obligation is paid. Complete information regarding devices can be found online.

" }} {{> news-item title="Planned Internet Outage, Sept. 3" message="

HCPSS has scheduled an internet outage from 9 a.m. - noon on Saturday, Sept. 3 for regular maintenance. During this time, the internet in HCPSS schools and offices will be out, and users will not be able to authenticate to many HCPSS online systems.

" }} {{> news-item title="Free Student RTA Bus Passes" message="

HCPSS again has partnered with Howard County Government and the Regional Transportation Agency of Central Maryland (RTA) to provide free RTA bus access to students, supporting those who use public transportation for work and other services in the county. They are not intended to take the place of school buses for transport to school.
