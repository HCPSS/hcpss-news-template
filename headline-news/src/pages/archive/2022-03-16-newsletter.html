Tents that were in place in the fall for outdoor eating at elementary schools will be reconstructed within the coming weeks to better facilitate outdoor dining. " }} {{> news-item title="Tentative Last Day of School" message="

Events

 {{> news-item title="HCASC Virtual Listen and Learn Sessions" message="

Wednesdays, March 16, 23, and 30, 7-8 p.m., virtual sessions

These sessions provide opportunities for students to discuss current topics of interest with students at other schools and school/school system leaders. Login details for each session are available online. Students must be logged in using the HCPSS account to attend and should attend the session for the area in which their school is located.

" }} {{> news-item title="Breaking Down the College Search Workshop" message="

Wednesday, March 23, 7 p.m., virtual workshop

Students in grades 11-12 are invited to attend this event to learn how to identify what college characteristics are most important for you and to develop an application that best demonstrates your strengths and match with an institution. This event is part of the HCPSS March to the Future series geared toward high school students as they begin to plan for life after graduation.

" }} {{> news-item title="HCASC Mental Health Panel" message="

Wednesday, March 23, 7-8:30 p.m., virtual event

The Howard County Association of Student Councils (HCASC) will host a virtual mental health panel for HCPSS middle and high school students. Panelists will answer questions about mental health support, which will be followed by a mental health-related discussion hosted by HCPSS’ Asian American Pacific Islander Advisory.

" }} {{> news-item title="Friends of Education Award" message="

Thursday, April 7, 4 p.m.

The Board of Education will recognize Ying Matties, founder of the Community Allies of Rainbow Youth (CARY) organization, with the Friends of Education Award during its meeting on Thursday, April 7. The Board will also acknowledge Friends of Education nominees Amanda Wang, Okinawan Karate Dojo and EnRich Minds for their contributions to the school system.
