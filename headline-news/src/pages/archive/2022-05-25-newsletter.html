If you ask an HCPSS physical education (PE) teacher what they teach, their answer may surprise you. It’s not just basketball or tennis or golf; it’s a whole range of life skills, like teamwork, sportsmanship, and leadership, that apply on and off the court. PE teachers also work hard to provide students with opportunities to discover activities that they can enjoy for a lifetime. Be sure to let the PE teacher(s) in your life know just how much you appreciate them!

 {{> news-item title="Students Earn an Associate Degree While in High School" message="

Eighteen current high school students will earn associates degrees before graduating high school, as part of the JumpStart dual enrollment program, a partnership between HCPSS and Howard Community College. Although thousands of Howard County students earn college credits before graduating high school, only a small percentage leave high school with their associate degree.

In 1985, HCPSS established a Teen Parenting Program to support the county’s teen parents and provide them with the tools and resources they would need to complete high school. Since then, HCPSS’ Teen Parenting Childcare and Outreach Programs have helped hundreds of students complete high school.
