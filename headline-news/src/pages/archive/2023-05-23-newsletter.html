Reading is a central part of every HCPSS student’s education. HCPSS’ reading specialists work behind the scenes at every HCPSS school, partnering with classroom teachers to provide students with the reading support they need to access instruction across content areas and be successful in the classroom and beyond.

" }} {{> news-item title="HCPSS Students to Participate in National Youth Orchestra Programs" message="

Four HCPSS students recently were selected to participate in Carnegie Hall’s prestigious National Youth Orchestra programs this summer. Three will participate in NYO2, a summer orchestral training program for outstanding American instrumentalists ages 14–17. The fourth will participate in the National Youth Orchestra of the United States of America (NYO-USA), a group consisting of the nation’s top musicians between the ages of 16 and 19.

" }} {{> news-item title="Families Must Register for Bus Service by June 1" message="

Families whose children are eligible for bus transportation must register through HCPSS Connect Synergy to receive bus service for the 2023-2024 school year. Instructions on how to register students are available online. " }} {{> news-item title="Provide Feedback on Inclement Weather Asynchronous Instruction Plan" message="

For the 2023-2024 school year, HCPSS again will repurpose up to three inclement weather days as asynchronous instruction days. All parents, students, staff and community members are invited to provide feedback on the plan through June 9.

" }} {{> news-item title="Important Health & Safety Message" message="

All HCPSS students and staff must stay home if they are experiencing symptom(s) associated with any communicable disease. Families, students and staff should perform daily health checks and stay home if they have symptoms of a communicable disease, including the seasonal flu, COVID-19 and Respiratory Syncytial Virus (RSV). Families are encouraged to consider taking advantage of flu and COVID vaccines and boosters.
