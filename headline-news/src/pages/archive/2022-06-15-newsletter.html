" }} {{> news-item title="Intern/Mentor Program Provides Unique Educational Experiences" message="

Open to high school juniors and seniors, the Gifted and Talented intern/mentor program is a year-long, credit-bearing course that enables students to work with a mentor in the community to identify a problem or question on a topic of the students’ choosing, conduct research, and make a meaningful contribution to the field of study.

" }} {{> news-item title="Bright Minds Grants to Support Innovative Classroom Projects" message="

Eighteen HCPSS educators have been awarded grants by the Bright Minds Foundation, the school system’s independent, nonprofit organization. The grants, totaling nearly $25,000, will be used to fund innovative instructional projects during the 2022-2023 school year.

" }} {{> news-item title="Enhanced Pre-K Program" message="

HCPSS will offer half- and full-day prekindergarten to eligible children at 17 schools across the county during the 2022-23 school year. These classes will be co-taught in inclusive classrooms with developmentally appropriate instruction and evidence-based intervention and include lunch/recess and related arts.

" }} {{> news-item title="ARL Teams Places Second in National Finance Competition" message="

The Applications and Research Lab Academy of Finance Team placed second in the 2022 National Personal Finance Challenge. This is the second consecutive year the team has achieved this distinction.

" }} {{> news-item title="Guilford ES Wins Second Green Flag Award" message="

For the second time, Guilford Elementary School has been awarded the National Wildlife Federation Eco-Schools USA Green Flag. The award recognizes GES’ exceptional achievement in educating for sustainability, improving the environmental footprint of the school grounds, facility and curriculum.

" }} {{> news-item title="Students’ Research and Advocacy Yields New Maryland Law" message="

In 2018, a group of Centennial Lane Elementary School students began investigating coal tar sealants. Now, five years later, the work of those students, now at Centennial High School, has directly led to a new Maryland law banning the use of coal tar sealants statewide.
